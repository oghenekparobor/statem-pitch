01 · Problem & demand

Crypto-paid freelancers, DeFi pros, and digital-nomad-visa applicants cannot prove income or assets to embassies, landlords, or lenders the way a traditional bank statement does. Exchange CSVs lack identity binding; blockchain explorers lack institutional formatting; tax tools (Koinly, CoinTracker) produce tax reports, not bank statements.

03 · Risk map

SeverityRisk
High · MarketInstitutional acceptance is unproven. UK gov explicitly says Bitcoin savings unacceptable for student visas. One Schengen consulate said fiat-only. A statem.finance PDF has no regulatory standing — institutions trust banks because banks are regulated, not because of cryptographic stamps. This is the existential question.
High · MarketSelf-custody vs centralized direction. Fannie Mae / Newrez only recognize crypto held at U.S.-regulated exchanges. Statem operates on self-custodial wallets. That is opposite of where institutional trust is currently extending — but also the gap nobody else fills.
Med · CompetitiveSAS could substitute. If institutions converge on "present a Civic / SAS attestation," the bank-format PDF becomes irrelevant. Mitigation: integrate with SAS as the issuer of a "verified financial statement" attestation. Ride the standard, don't fight it.
Med · TechnicalHistorical price accuracy across 7 chains. Birdeye covers Solana well; CoinGecko handles EVMs but historical prices for long-tail tokens at exact tx timestamps are brittle. A wrong USD figure is grounds for institutional rejection.
Med · DistributionNo identified channel. Visa agencies, immigration consultants, and crypto-paid remote workers are dispersed audiences. Without a partner channel, paid acquisition will likely cost more than $9–19 LTV.
Med · RegulatoryKYC data handling. Compliance claims (GDPR, NDPR, POPIA) need a DPA, retention policy, and audited right-to-erasure flow before paid customers — not legal blockers, but legal homework.
Low · TechnicalCryptographic stamp is web2-grade. SHA-256 stored in Postgres. A determined adversary controlling Statem's DB can forge records. Anchoring to Solana (memo program or SAS attestation) hardens the integrity claim — cheap and material.

04 · Crypto-necessity check

"What breaks if you remove the blockchain?"

Most of it. The product is a wrapper over on-chain data — Helius/RPC for Solana, Alchemy for EVM, Blockstream for Bitcoin. Without these, there is no statement. KYC binding to a wallet is meaningful only because the wallet is the on-chain identity. The cryptographic stamp could exist for any document but has highest value when it can be cross-verified against on-chain history. Crypto is load-bearing, not ornamental.

Recommendation: strengthen the crypto-native claim. Replace pure-DB SHA-256 with a Solana memo-program anchor (~$0.0001/stmt). Add an SAS attestation issued at KYC completion. Then the integrity story is "verifiable on-chain" rather than "trust our database."

06 · Next steps (4-week plan)

  1. Week 1 — Acceptance test (existential). Generate 5–10 sample statements for friendly users and physically submit them to: a Schengen consulate, a Portuguese/Spanish landlord agency, a UK student-visa caseworker, and one US immigration attorney. Outcome metric: accepted / accepted-as-supplementary / rejected. This single test resolves whether Statem has product-market fit or just product.
  2. Week 1 — B2B partner outreach (parallel). Email 20 immigration consultants and 10 crypto-tax firms. Offer Statem at $0 for their first 10 client statements in exchange for a 30-min feedback call. Goal: find a channel partner.
  3. Week 2 — SAS integration. Issue a Statem attestation via Solana Attestation Service after KYC + statement generation. Anchors Statem in the canonical Solana identity stack.
  4. Week 2 — On-chain stamp anchor. Replace pure-DB SHA-256 with a Solana memo-program tx containing the hash. Cost: ~$0.0001/statement. Benefit: tamper claim is mathematically true, not "trust-our-DB" true.
  5. Week 3 — Distribution test. $200 of paid X ads targeting "digital nomad visa" / "USDC freelancer." Single CTA → landing page. If CAC > $50, kill paid; double down on B2B.
  6. Ongoing — Pricing experiment. $9/statement is in the noise vs. visa fees ($150–500). Test a $29 single tier with a "rush 24h" badge; institutional buyers don't bargain on compliance docs.
  7. Defer: Phase 4 (CBDC bridges, embassy portal integrations, Statem Score). 2027 problems. Stay focused on the acceptance test.

07 · Phase handoff

Verdict go-with-caveats. Recommended next phase: Build (light) → Validate (heavy). The product is shipped enough; the unknown is institutional acceptance, which can only be resolved by submitting real PDFs to real institutions. After the week-1 acceptance test, re-score and decide whether to invest in distribution, pivot the format, or pivot the audience (e.g., from individual visa applicants to crypto-payroll companies needing aggregated statements for tax).
