Problem02 / 12
Problem

If your money lives on-chain, the world acts like you don't have any.

Embassies, landlords, lenders, immigration officers, and tax authorities all ask the same question: where's the bank statement? Crypto-native earners have no answer that institutions accept — so they fake one, every month, by hand.

The painful workaround

Off-ramp monthly to a bank for 3–6 months before you apply. Eat the spread. Eat the fees. Eat the wait. Print a stamped statement. Pray it gets accepted.

What's broken

Exchange CSVs lack identity. Block explorers are jargon. Tax tools (Koinly, CoinTracker) produce tax reports — not bank statements. None bind a verified human to a wallet.

"Every month I have to up-ramp, put 2-3K in the bank to create a proof of income. So I have to choose the right bank — like, support me. I have to prepare 3 months before."

David · Vietnamese builder · Network School Malaysia

"I had to go to the bank physically. Download the statement, stamp it, send it over. It is a good day's effort."

Paresh · Indian founder · won 2-3 Solana hackathons

Crypto income is ordinary income at FMV

The IRS treats crypto received as compensation, staking, mining, or airdrops as ordinary income at fair market value on receipt — reported on W-2 or 1099, taxed exactly like wages.

The gap nobody fills

Recognition is broad. Self-custodial recognition is not. Mortgage acceptance only covers crypto at U.S.-regulated exchanges. Visa officers want bank-shaped paper. Auditors want signed attestations. Self-custodial wallet holders — the entire native crypto economy — still have no path. Statem is the bridge: take a self-custodial wallet, bind a verified human to it, produce a document the legacy world can read.

Solution04 / 12
Solution

One PDF. KYC-bound. Cryptographically stamped. Verifiable in a browser.

How it works

  1. Connect wallet — Solana live today; EVM (5 chains) + Bitcoin scaffolded for Q3
  2. Choose period — 1M, 3M, 6M, 1Y, custom, or all-time
  3. Statem fetches & enriches via Helius, Alchemy, Birdeye, Blockstream
  4. One-time KYC via Didit — only the hash binds wallet → human
  5. SHA-256 stamp embedded in PDF; on-chain anchor coming
  6. Public verify portal — institutions check tampering at verify.statem.finance/[id]

Individual one-offs are the wedge — Justine flagged that LTV is short. Travel agencies and crypto-native enterprises are the engine: both are recurring, both have volume, and both have B2B-grade willingness to pay. x402 lets enterprises pay per API call without a contract — a Solana-native primitive that meets crypto-native finance teams where they already operate.
